ABOUT TEARS

TEARS is a social organization formed to help the street children to regain their joy and security in their lives. The organization has been started its functions a few years back picking up children from Railway platforms who were facing various problems due to extreme poverty, exploitation and stigma.

TEARS operate a Drop – In – Centre just opposite to Howrah Railway Station, adjacent to Howrah Station Post office where we take care of the street children addicted to solvents around Howrah Rly. Station and try our best to bring them back to the main stream of the society.

Smt. Gita Routh has played a vital role with her strong will power and dedication of service and we happy to say that some children at present here, who have changed their previous life style and now monitoring other street children in the Drop – In –Centre Howrah.

Children often turn to the street because of child abuse or neglect – some are wayward and run away. The consequence of street children are teenage pregnancy, higher risks of sexually transmitted deseares, antisocial activities, begging, prostitution and death. Open air bathing of street children is a very common scene in India. In a study conducted on platform and working children of Howrah Railway Station. it has been found that a large number of children are involved into various crimes and sexually abused and exposed – The girls have no idea of safe – sex and these children are being deprived of the four basic Rights guaranteed by the UN convention 1989 i.e, Awareness / Detoxification / Rehabitation and After Care.

We have in our mind Mira Nair’s Academy Award – nominated film Salam Bombay (1988) about Mumbai’s underbelly prostitution and street children. It also used street children as actors. We also strongly believe the safe guard of the human rights of the street children and that’s why we demand strong implementation of Juvenile Justice Act in police lockup and jails and amendment of sec. 53 and sec. 54 of CrPC to make medical examination mandatory. We strongly believe that these street children can be brought back to the main stream of the society thro’ our vigorous persuasion which may somehow cause reduction of corruption in the society in future.
